## POST 3 — The Vendor Side (Hook: unexpected angle, empathy)

> Most "haggling tips" articles teach you how to pay as little as possible.
>
> That bothers me.
>
> A leather bag in Morocco — the real one, hand-stitched, full grain —
> takes about 8 hours to make. The artisan earns roughly $13/day minimum wage.
>
> At 8 hours of labour + materials, a *fair* price is around $30–40.
>
> Yet I've seen travel blogs celebrate getting these bags for $12.
>
> That's not smart haggling. That's exploitation with a selfie attached.
>
> I'm building Haggle to fix this from both sides:
>
> For travellers — know what's fair, know what's a rip-off
> For vendors — get a free page to share your craft story and set context
>
> The goal isn't to help people pay minimum. It's to help people pay *fair*.
> Fair means the vendor covers their costs, earns a living, and respects their
> own work. Fair means the traveller doesn't feel robbed.
>
> That middle ground exists. We just need the data to find it.
>
> If you've ever bought something at a market and wondered "did I just screw
> someone over?" — this app is for you.
